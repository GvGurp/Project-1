What is a BSA?

BSA stands for binding study advice. BSA is a decision made by a college or university about the progress of your school education. The college or university decides if the advice they gave you is a binding study advice or just study advice. A BSA is bound by rules.If you think that the BSA that was given to you doesnâ€™t fit these rules, you can object.

BSA rules

Each student gets a BSA at the end of their first school year.The BSA can be negative and positive.Depending on the policy at your college/university, you can be forced to stop your education there if your BSA is negative.

The rules that have to be followed when a BSA is given are down below.

1. You must receive sufficient study guidance from a study counselor.
2. You must be warned in good time about a bad BSA, so that you have a reasonable amount of time to improve your study results.When issuing this warning, the program must take into account the so-called 1 february rule. This prevents the termination of the study from having consequences for the student's finance .
3. The school must take into account personal circumstances such as special family circumstances, a disability or a participation council.
4. You have the right to be heard.
